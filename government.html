Government Impersonation Scams

What Are Government Impersonation Scams?

Government impersonation scams involve fraudsters posing as officials or representatives of government agencies to deceive individuals or businesses into sending money or sharing sensitive information.

These scams often rely on fear, authority, and urgency, using threats of fines, arrest, or legal action to pressure victims into acting quickly.

Common Types of Government Impersonation Scams

Tax and Revenue Authority Impersonation

Scammers pose as tax agencies and claim unpaid taxes, penalties, or audits. Victims are pressured to make immediate payments to avoid legal consequences.

Law Enforcement Impersonation

Fraudsters impersonate police officers or federal agents, threatening arrest or investigation unless payments or personal details are provided.

Immigration and Border Services Scams

Victims receive fake notices claiming visa issues, deportation risks, or documentation problems that require urgent payment or verification.

Benefit and Grant Fraud

Scammers claim victims are eligible for government benefits or grants but request fees or personal information to “process” the application.

Fake Government Communications

Fraudulent emails, letters, or phone calls mimic official government formats and logos to appear legitimate and gain trust.
