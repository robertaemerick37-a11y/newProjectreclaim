Pig-Butchering Scams

What Are Pig-Butchering Scams?

Pig-butchering scams are long-term fraud schemes where scammers build trust with victims over time before manipulating them into sending money. These scams often combine emotional manipulation with fake investment opportunities.

The term “pig-butchering” refers to how scammers gradually gain trust, encourage repeated investments, and increase financial pressure before abruptly disappearing with the victim’s funds.

Common Types of Pig-Butchering Scams

Relationship-Based Grooming

Scammers initiate contact through social media, dating platforms, or messaging apps, forming personal or romantic relationships to establish trust over weeks or months.

Fake Investment Platforms

Victims are directed to professional-looking investment platforms that display fabricated profits. These platforms are controlled by scammers and prevent legitimate withdrawals.

Gradual Escalation of Deposits

Initial investments may be small to build confidence. Over time, victims are encouraged to deposit increasingly larger amounts using persuasive success stories or false guarantees.

Withdrawal Restrictions and Fee Demands

When victims attempt to withdraw funds, scammers demand additional payments for taxes, verification, or account unlocking—none of which result in successful withdrawals.

Sudden Disappearance

Once victims are unable or unwilling to send more money, communication abruptly ends and access to platforms or accounts is cut off.
