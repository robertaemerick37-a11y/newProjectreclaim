About Us

Falling victim to an online scam can be overwhelming and confusing. At GetReclaim, we support individuals affected by online financial fraud by helping them understand what steps to take next. We provide guidance on filing complaints, recognizing scam tactics, and navigating available options after a scam has occurred. Our focus is on clarity, awareness, and practical support—so victims are not left to navigate the process alone. As you explore the types of scams we address below, we hope you find the information relevant to your experience and the guidance you are looking for.

Scams We Deal With

Cryptocurrency Scams

In recent years, cryptocurrency scam projects have increased, requiring greater awareness and caution.

Investment Scams

Countless individuals engage in investment activities, only to fall victim to fraudulent schemes.

Forex Scams

Forex scams target eager traders, but we help expose schemes and safeguard your investments.

Binary Scams

Deceptive binary options brokers emerge yearly — we work to protect victims from these schemes.

Phishing Fraud

Phishing scams targeting financial data evolve constantly, but we help victims recover and respond.

Real Estate Scam

From rental scams to fraudulent property purchases, we assist in combating property fraud.

Romance Scams

Fraudsters build fake emotional relationships online to gain trust, then manipulate victims into sending money or financial information.

Pig-Butchering Scams

These long-term schemes combine emotional manipulation with fake investment opportunities , gradually convincing victims to commit larger sums of money.

Marketplace Scams

Scammers exploit online marketplaces by advertising fake products, demanding upfront payments, or disappearing after a transaction is completed.

Business Email Compromise

Criminals impersonate executives, employees , or trusted partners to trick businesses into sending payments or sensitive financial information.

Tax-related scams

Tax scams involve fraudulent messages claiming unpaid taxes , refunds, or penalties, pressuring victims to act quickly or provide personal details.

Government impersonation(IRS,SSA,Medicare)

Scammers pose as government agencies to threaten legal action, demand payments, or request sensitive information under false authority.
